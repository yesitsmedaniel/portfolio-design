ABOUT


Hey again, Daniel here. I'm the design manager at City Picture Frame in San Francisco.


My team works with designers, galleries, artists and corporate clients; always working to come up with project solutions—from budget, to timeline, to nailing the right aesthetic. To do this I have to ask the right questions—not just what they want, but why? Where’s it getting installed? How do they want it to fit into their space? Who’s going to see it? (“When” always comes up too.)

To do good design I think you always have to dig into those details. Understanding our client’s needs, visions, and concerns is key to our design process.





So how’d I get here? I’ve been designing since I was little. As a kid I would fill sketchbooks with comic book drawings, create my own VHS packaging for my favorite movies, and endlessly study video game instructional booklets. Fast-forward—after studying Fine Art in college, I took a role as print designer at a shoe company. As I learned on the fly, I fell in love with typography. While I never quite got the hang of women’s fashion footwear, I leveled-up as a visual communicator and designer.

Moving to San Francisco, I took up custom framing– a secretly grueling world of designing on the fly, tough deadlines, and delivering for people who put their trust in you. I learned to be patient, listen, and really understand where people are coming from, and what they are trying to achieve. I love collaborating with new people, hashing out the details, and solving problems with my visual instincts and analytical point of view. I could see firsthand how design could take a problem, flip it on its head, and ultimately create a positive and shareable experience with others.

Now I’m ready to level up again—I’m finishing the User Experience Design program at Bloc, and I want to tackle new projects that make a real difference; whether that’s streamlining business interactions, learning a simple way to budget, or just pairing the right wine & cheese. Let me know if you have a project or role that I could help with!
